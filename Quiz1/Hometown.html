Where Industry
and Nature Thrive

Bontang is not only an industrial powerhouse but also a city blessed with natural charm. It is home to PT Badak LNG, once the world’s largest liquefied natural gas processing plant, and PT Pupuk Kalimantan Timur, the biggest fertilizer producer in Southeast Asia. Despite this industrial dominance, Bontang proudly carries the title of “Kota Taman”. The name is an acronym for Terbit, Agamis, Mandiri, Aman, Nyaman (Bright, Religious, Independent, Safe, and Comfortable). This vision is reflected in its well-planned green spaces and initiatives that emphasize harmony between industry and environment.

Beyond its industrial identity, Bontang offers breathtaking attractions such as Beras Basah Island with its crystal-clear waters, the stilt village of Bontang Kuala, and Kutai National Park, a vital sanctuary for orangutans. For me, visiting Bontang feels like stepping into a city where contrasts unite. You can witness world-class industry in the morning, spend the afternoon enjoying fresh seafood by the sea, and walk through mangrove forests at sunset. It is a place that shows how industry and nature, when balanced, can create something truly inspiring.
